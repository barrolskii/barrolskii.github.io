    A few things I think new programmers should know

    JUL. 24, 2021

    I've been programmer for about 5-6 years now and I've run into problems that aren't always directly programming related but have affected me when programming. I was thinking about these issues and I thought to myself it would've been really good to know that how I felt or the problems I was running into are normal and that I shouldn't stress about them. So this is what this blog post is, a post about non-programming issues related to programming that I wish I knew about throughout my programming journey. Just as a side note I call these problems non-programming problems because some of them apply to other things apart from programming but this will still be a programming focused post. Anyway let's begin!

    Take Your Time When Learning Something New

    I've noticed a trend on YouTube where people try to challenge themselves and try to do a project in a specific amount of time. "I LEARNED X IN A WEEK", "RECREATING Y IN 2 DAYS" etc. It is impressive when someone does that but I don't think it's a good attitude to have when trying to learn and understand something new and it's really not a healthy attitude to have towards all learning. Everyone learns at their own pace. Some people are faster than others and that's okay. What matters most when you're learning something new is that you actually understand what you're learning. I've run into this problem myself when I was researching scripting languages. I took a week off work at the time and said that I was going to complete the write an interpreter in Go book within that week. That was the challenge for myself and I tried very hard to stick to it. No surprise to anyone I didn't complete the project within a week (I think it took me 9-10 days) and the worst part about it was when I did complete the project there were gaps in my knowledge about the subject due to me rushing through things. There is nothing wrong with taking your time when learning something new and if it takes you a long time that doesn't mean it is a bad thing. Sometimes you'll pick things up quickly and other times you won't. Don't put pressure on yourself and don't compare what you do and how you learn with other people or these YouTube videos of people learning things in a short amount of time. Learning should be fun and enjoyable and putting pressure on yourself trying to speed run learning a new and/or complex topic will only make the experience worse for you. Tackle the task at a pace you are comfortable with. You're learning this for yourself and not other people.

    Take A Break

    I'm guilty of not doing this now but take regular breaks from your computer and know when to stop. Countless times I've run into a bug when I've been coding all day and I think to myself "I'll fix this and call it a day". That doesn't always work. So many times have I spent needless hours torturing myself trying to fix an issue when I'm tried and fatigued from working all day when the best thing to do in that situation is to just call it a day. Remember the bug isn't going to fix itself overnight. It'll still be there when you wake up tomorrow. You will probably find as well you'll fix the bug quite quickly when you have a good nights rest and come back to the problem. Don't forget to take regular breaks as well when you're working at a computer. There are a whole host of health issues that occur when working at a computer and simply getting up and walking a short distance and having a stretch every 2 hours does wonders for you. We're not made to spend obscene hours sat down behind a desk. Take regular breaks. You physical and mental health will thank you for doing so. You'll also be able to take a step back from any issues you've run into and come back to it with a fresh mind. Take a break. It's good for you.

    Do Other Things That Don't Involve Computers

    This is a simple one but make sure you have other hobbies that don't involve being on a computer. Programming is a hobby of mine and also my career and I love playing games as well but it's not good to spend most of your time in front of a screen (trust me). Before lock down I used to snowboard regularly, go to the gym, read, and meet up with my friends. Not exactly super exciting stuff but it allowed me to actually be active and to see people. Get out and get some other hobbies even if it is just reading a book and going for walks. There's nothing wrong with taking a break from the computer.

    Project Block

    This is a more difficult one because this is different from person to person but project block sucks when you want to start a new project but you have no clue what to do. I've ran into this myself when I was a very inexperienced programmer where I wanted to do something cool but I didn't have the knowledge or any ideas of what specifically to do. I don't have a specific answer of what you should do but what I would recommend is using this rut to either progress your knowledge of a programming language, look at other projects people have done and try to do your own take on them, or find a problem that you have and try to make a program to fix it. Even if it is something simple it's good to do something and let your mind figure out what to do next. Also when you have project ideas note them down somewhere so when you don't know what to do you can look at your list of projects and pick one.

    Don't Compare Yourself To Others

    I find this is good to do in general and not just with programming but don't compare yourself to other people. That's a really easy way to make yourself feel down and inadequate. People will always do awesome things and you should use that as inspiration for yourself but don't look at other peoples achievements and think "I've done nothing compared to them" or "Why bother when there are people so much better than me?". You'll never be able to improve yourself when you're too busy comparing yourself to other people. Look where you are now compared to where you started. If you're not happy with your progress think why and try to push yourself a bit more so you can improve. Comparing yourself with others is an easy way to put unnecessary pressure on yourself. More than likely you're just putting to much pressure on yourself in the first place and that's why you're comparing yourself to others. Relax and take a step back. You're doing just fine.

    Also something that is related to this is if you don't know something that is not a weakness and asking others for help is not a weakness. It's great to want to figure out things on your own but when you really do get stuck and don't understand something ask for help. Wanting to gain more knowledge and better yourself is not a bad thing and people who make you think so are not nice people. Don't forget as well that people who know more than you about something are not a threat. You can learn from these people and better yourself from what they've learned. Do that. You can't do everything by yourself.

    Do Difficult Things To Get Better

    When I look back at my second year of university I really didn't consider myself to be a good programmer. I lived the typical student lifestyle in my first year and I was really behind the top students in my class in both skill and knowledge because I wasn't putting the time in with personal projects. So when I was on placement I gave myself projects and tasks only on Linux so I could learn a completely new tool set and to make sure I really understood what I was doing. My knowledge and skill as a programmer improved so much that I now actually work with the same people who were at the top of my class in university. If I didn't challenge myself and give myself projects that were difficult and tested my knowledge then I wouldn't have improved. I didn't try to undertake projects that I found difficult just to say I could but because I wanted to be good at what I do and to have the knowledge to back myself up. I would recommend that you do that too. If there is a project that you think oh I'm not good enough to do that you should do it anyway. If something is difficult it means it will push you to be your best and if you don't have the knowledge to tackle the task properly then you can find areas where your knowledge is lacking. If everything is easy then you don't have room to improve. Challenge yourself and push to improve and in time you'll look back and notice how much progress you have made.

    Hopefully some of that helps out. Everyone's learning journey is different so do what works best for you. This is stuff that would've helped me when I was putting too much pressure on myself and not feeling good enough. Remember to relax and don't be too hard on yourself. Everyone feels that way every once in a while. Just keep going and try your best.
