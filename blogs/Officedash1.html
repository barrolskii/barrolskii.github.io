    My first solo Unreal Engine project

    OCT. 08, 2020

    A few weeks ago I started a project in Unreal. I decided I was going to make a game but to make sure that I don't spend too much time jumping down rabbit holes I gave myself a deadline to finish the project in under 2 weeks. I managed to finish the project well under the deadline, albeit the project did have a small scope and wasn't exactly hard. That's besides the point though. I made my first game in Unreal and I'm quite happy with it.

    The Game

    Office Dash is an 2.5D infinite runner. You play as a disgruntled office worker who is tired of working a 9-5 job, being cooped up all day indoors, and feeling underappreciated for their work. One day you decide you've had enough of the tedium so you decide to quit your job in the most dramatic way possible. By jumping out of the nearest window and running across the rooftops of adjacent buildings into the sunset. Along the way you collect employee of the month trophies from other offices so you can finally have something to show for all your hard work. In game these trophies serve as another way for you to get points to increase your score.

    The Inspiration

    There wasn't a direct inspiration for this game. I kind of just decided one day that I was going to make an infinite runner in Unreal. The last time I actually messed about in a game engine for fun or to learn something new in my own time was back during my second year of college when I was learning to program. I used to make small little games (the vast majority of which went unfinished I'll admit) in the Love2D engine. I used to have a lot of fun with the engine and it was one of the factors that helped me choose to go on to study games programming at university.

    I noticed I stopped having so much fun with learning game engines when I had to use one for my group project module in my second year of university. Along with having a typical student life in first year (partying a lot) and working on tests and assessments I got out of messing about and doing small projects in a game engine just for fun. I realised I really missed it and as great as my university course is, it'll never be as much fun as if you decide to work on a project yourself.

    So I decided I'd get back into games engines but this time not on the lower level technical side that my course covers ()although I do enjoy that don't get me wrong) and this time round I'd use a more professional standard engine. It was a toss up between Unity and Unreal but in the end I chose Unreal as if a project does get me to write some C++, I'd rather do that than C#. Nothing against C# but my personal preference out of those two is C++.

    I found that the best part of this project was not only having fun throughout but the satisfaction of seeing a project to the end and watching it progress. I really did miss making games. It was like being back in college again. Except this time I'm not a complete noob and I have a general idea of what I'm doing.

    Future Support

    During the development of the game I kept getting ideas of more features to add to the game but I haven't implemented as of yet just because I didn't want to feature creep. If I didn't stop myself I'd probably still be working on the project. I'm currently working on a new patch for the game, version 1.1.0 is in the works and will be done very soon so I'll make a blog post about that with the changelog. I would like to put this game up on itch.io or maybe Steam one day but sadly at the moment I can't.

    The reason I can't release the game (when I can it will be out for free) is because of the background music I'm using. I'm using a song called 20xx by Evan King. The song is under the creative commons license but on his website he says to get in touch with him if you want to use his music for things like games, films, etc. I've tried to get in contact with him but I've had no luck. Creative commons or not I still want to respect the artist and the work he's done and I don't want to release the game until I get the go ahead from him. So until then we'll just have to wait and see and hopefully he gives me the go ahead. Anyway, here's some screenshots of the game.
