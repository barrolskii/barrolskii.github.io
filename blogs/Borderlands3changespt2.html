    Some more changes I'd like to see

    AUG. 03, 2020

    I've taken a break from playing Borderlands 3 and in recent weeks I've picked the game back up. The last time I played I wasn't happy with the current state of the game and I thought there's no point in playing it if it's just going to cause me frustration. I think the last time I played was during the cartel event. I found that event to be fun but I found the overall experience to be lacklustre and a lot of the problems that the game had either at launch or a few patches after launch to still persist. So I thought now is probably a good time to give my options on the current state of the game and how I think certain aspects could be improved.

    Mayhem mode

    Mayhem mode has been overhauled in an update called Mayhem 2.0. 1.0 wasn't a great experience and the addition of mayhem 4 killed a lot of character builds making them unplayable late game and destroyed end game weapon balancing, causing Gearbox to buff weapons to be stupidly powerful. Mayhem 2.0 was meant to fix these problems, allowing for more end game build diversity, different modifiers that make the game more interesting, and even more choice in which weapons players want to use at end game. In my opinion they have not achieved this.

    I'll start with one thing they did decently well and that was the mêlée scaling and the action skill damage scaling. Mêlée builds and using you action skill is actually viable in end game now. I'm very happy to hear about and see builds of this in game as this is a step in the right direction for end game build diversity. Letting players play the way they want is always a good thing. What they haven't done is the same for weapons. When mayhem 2.0 first came out a big complaint was that it was basically the same end game as Borderlands 2 where only a few specific weapons were viable and every build had to have them. They made some changes in the next few patches which allowed for a few more weapons to be viable at mayhem level 10 (the highest difficulty) but I found that unless you were using top tier weapons then you won't be able to play at the highest difficulty

    One of my favourite builds to play in the game is an assault rifle Moze build, where you utilise bottomless mags, no iron bear, and use different assault rifles of the same manufacturer (all with different elements) to mow through hordes of enemies. I have two guns I like to use in particular, the Kaos, and the Warlord. I managed to pick up a mayhem 10 version of both of those weapons and found that they just didn't have the stopping power to be viable at that difficulty. I've tried using other weapons as well like the Maggie, Lyuda, the 9 vold, but hardly any of them pack a punch when fighting enemies at end game. I now use the Flipper and Light show (like almost everyone in the player base) because they're the easiest guns to get and they do insane amounts of damage. I'm so jaded when it comes to following the meta weapons in Borderlands because I did it for 7 years in Borderlands 2. And to be quite honest if I'm going to be doing the same thing as I did in that game I might as well go back and play that game because it has far better replay value than 3 and you can mod the game.

    Mayhem modifiers

    With the addition of mayhem 2.0 came the addition of new modifiers. You have no choice but to play with these at end game and if you want the mayhem exclusive drops but don't want the hassle of these modifiers you're fresh out of luck. Hearing the complains of the first set of modifiers, this time Gearbox allows you to randomly role modifiers for your mayhem experience. Not choose what modifiers you want but randomly role them and hope you get the modifiers you want. I think that is such a stupid system to implement and is a massive waste of players time. No matter what people are going to want to play with the modifiers that benefits them the most so if you allowed players to pick and choose individual modifiers they'll just choose the optimal ones. Allowing for random roles just forces players to endure through modifiers that they don't like and if they don't want to endure they'll have to waste time rolling modifiers hoping they get the one's that they want.

    I find the selection of modifiers available to be very poor and don't make the game a more enjoyable experience. Big head mode is straight from the 90s and I think that modifier should stay there. Okay it makes critical shots easier to hit but it just doesn't feel like it should be in the game. Big head mode is funny when you are a kid for a couple of seconds but then the joke stops being funny. Buddy system is another poor choice in modifiers but it's also one of the best to get. Some enemies will spawn with a robot that makes them invulnerable (great balance right?) and the only way to stop them from being invulnerable is to kill the buddy system. Only problem is the buddy system doesn't have collision enabled and so can clip through the geometry. Meaning you have to wait there like a lemon for the robot to fly out of the geometry to be hit. Another dumb modifier is the -75% critical hit damage modifier. This completely kills Fl4k's critical hit builds and means if you can kill enemies with body shots don't even bother. It makes enemies even spongier than they already are and that is a poor decision to make when their health values have been increased by 10,000%! There are plenty more modifiers I don't like and think don't add anything to the game but those are the ones that come to me off the top of my head.

    Well there's no point in complaining about these things if I don't offer some sort of suggestion to improve. Personally I think they should get rid of the modifiers altogether. Unless they want to change it to where they are completely optional and they have added benefits (increased quality of loot drops, bosses have a higher chance to drop their dedicated loot) then I really don't see the point in having them in the game. I also believe that the health scaling should go. I think instead of buffing health values by 10,000%, you should base the difficulty off of players accuracy and element matching abilities. What I mean by this is, for mayhem 1-5, damage to enemies bodies should be lowered but critical hits should either remain the same or offer slightly increased damage. The damage reduction in non-critical hits would cap out at mayhem 5 but the skill then would be based off of players accuracy. Then for mayhem levels 6-10 you will do less damage for not matching elements. So if you try and kill an enemy with body shots with a shock weapon against flesh, then you'll have a really tough time. But if you hit your crits and use a fire weapon against the flesh target then you'll be rewarded with a quick kill. This means you only have to balance the damage reduction modifiers so that they feel fair and means you don't have to completely overhaul the current weapon balance system. If you want to make sure players are also farming for gear with mayhem levels attached to them then add another damage resistance modifier that reduces damage based on the mayhem level of the gun.

    DLC quality

    So far Gearbox has released three full DLC packs for the game and honestly I think so far they have all been great. I've enjoyed each one so far and I was pleasantly surprised with the newest bounty of blood DLC. The free events on the other hand I find have not been up to par. The cartel event was fun but there is still the massive issue of them being timed events. I will keep complaining about it until they do it but, they need to make them permanent additions to the game and when the events come back around just add more content to them! I'll happily pay for them just like the head hunter packs in Borderlands 2 but right now, locking content away in a PvE game to seasons I find insulting. Gearbox really need to sort themselves out there.

    I think overall the game is in a better place than when I last played it but I still don't think it's in the state it should be. There are still a weapons that Gearbox haven't put into the loot pool since launch, there's still not much reason to keep playing at end game, and overall re-playability is nowhere near to where Borderlands 2 is. I'm very hopeful that the game will get there and these are just bumps in the road to greatness. I complain about the problems of this game because I love the Borderlands series and I want it to be a great experience. But overall Gearbox really need to figure out which direction they want to take this game. Borderlands 2 was just about having fun, Randy Pitchford himself said they didn't include some mechanics in 2 from Borderlands 1 because they weren't fun. Having the main priority as fun for Borderlands 2 worked for them. I think they need to do this for Borderlands 3 otherwise it won't last nearly as long a 2 did.
