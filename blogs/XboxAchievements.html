    A talk about the current state of achievements

    DEC. 21, 2020

    A few days ago I finished the first semester of my final year at university and decided to boot up my Xbox One and play a few games to unwind. I've been swapping between Peggle 2, Lego Star Wars 2, and Gears of War 2 and I've noticed that getting achievements on the old Xbox 360 games is way more satisfying than getting achievements on the Xbox One games.

    So How Are They More Satisfying?

    Of course this is simply my opinion on the matter and I'll be up front about this but I do have a bias towards the Xbox 360. I grew up playing the Xbox 360 and have fond memories of the console and the games I played while growing up. I'm sure there are kids and adults alike now who are going to get an Xbox Series X or S for Christmas, see that first achievement pop, and love it as I first did.

    Now there are a few things I found that I didn't enjoy about the current achievement system and the first thing is the notification sound. I find the current achievement notification sound to be too long and too drawn out. To me it seems like the sound is trying to make a big deal about you unlocking an achievement regardless of how long it took you to earn or how hard the achievement was. The Xbox 360 sound on the other hand I found is short but long enough to grab your attention. The little ping (or bleep bloop if you watch Red Vs Blue) sound immediately grabs my attention and more often than not I'm disappointed to see a friend coming online for the 6th time that evening and not an achievement but when it is an achievement, from hearing that notification I want to see what I've just earned.

    Another thing I found that I dislike is the inclusion of rare achievements. One the Xbox One (and the Series S/X now) there are achievements that are marked as rare which will have a little diamond next to them to denote their rarity. When you unlock one of these achievements you also get a little diamond animation and a different notification sound which shows that you've unlocked an extra special achievement. Now an achievement can only be marked as rare if less than 10% of the community for that game have an achievement. This sounds good in practice but you'll quickly find that most of the achievements on a games achievement list are rare. This takes the magic away for what was supposed to be kind of a big deal. Now I do like the stats on achievements that Microsoft added with this. I find it interesting to see what percentage of people got certain achievements and I like the bragging rights that come with some achievements. If you got an achievement that only 0.01% of the community of that game got then you should be proud of that. Overall I found the rare achievement system a good idea in theory but in practice it falls apart.

    The next issue I found is when you get 100% of all achievements in a game. With the Xbox One all you get is a little message that says well done when you go onto the completed games achievement list. You get nothing else (aside from bragging rights). PlayStation awards you a platinum trophy that you can add to your trophy count but on Xbox you don't get anything which I find to be extremely disappointing. When you got 100% of the achievements on Xbox 360 you actually got something. Now granted this is very limited and I'm pretty sure only you can see it but you got a little badge which was simply just the icon for the game added to your dashboard. If you open the menu icon to the right of play game from the dashboard where you can view your achievements you will see at the start a number showing the total amount of games you've completed along with the badges for each game. A simple thing but honestly I found it so satisfying to look at when I got 100% of the achievements for a game. Its like my little trophy wall of completed games.

    This last point is filled with nostalgia and bias but I still want to put it on here and that is I don't really like the achievement pop animation on the Xbox One. One thing I do want to say is that I think it's great that you can customise the colour of the achievement pop. More user customisation is hardly ever a bad thing and the more power to the user the better in my opinion. But it still doesn't change the fact that personally I don't like the basic or the rare achievement pop animation. I prefer the good old Xbox 360 animation.

    Suggestions For Improvements

    So I do have suggestions for the issues that I talked about. The first suggestion I have is to allow us to change the sound and the animation for achievement pops. Simply add the Xbox 360 achievement notification sound and the animation and allow us to choose that instead of just being able to change the colour. I think this is a trivial change to add and it will go a long way for us 360 players who want to bring that nostalgia with us to the new generation.

    Another suggestion I have is addition of an achievement wall on our Xbox profiles. I'm thinking of a system similar to how Steam does it where you can show off the achievements you're most proud of and also allow you to show off what games you've completed alongside them. So not only can you show off your greatest achievements but you can show off your greatest completions as well.

    An issue I would like to see addressed as well is unobtainable achievements in games. When a game has multiplayer achievements they are usually only obtainable as long as the games servers are up. But when the servers go down and you can't host games locally you lose out forever in earning those achievements. There are also single player games that have unobtainable achievements which were never patched and will never be able to be unlocked unless someone cheats and risks getting their account banned. I personally think that if a developer makes achievements unobtainable either directly or indirectly, those achievements should be given to players. Now I don't think they should just be given over night to everyone that has the game. I know people don't really like the idea of this when they have earned achievements that are no longer available but they did at the time you could get them. I think that there should be an Xbox app available on the Xbox marketplace where you can choose to redeem the unobtainable achievements. That way if people want to get them they can and if people want to leave the achievements and not be given to them then they can choose to not get the app and the achievements will remain locked on their profile. Microsoft created achievements for Xbox and I believe (in my ignorance here, I know nothing about the achievement interface) that they have the power to implement a system like this. Achievements are fun to get when you get into it and finding out that the are loads of achievements that are impossible to get ruins the fun with a lot of games. This is a problem that can be fixed.

    The last suggestion and I kind of mentioned it in the last one is the inclusion of or extension of the little badges you could get for completing a game in the Xbox 360. Quite simply having some reward at the end of a completion other than just a message saying well done you got 100% of the achievements. I think having a badge at the end of a completion to show off and having a viewable list of them like in the Xbox 360 dashboard would go a long way.

    Actually no I tell a lie this is the last suggestion. I'd like to be able to sort achievements by base game and individual DLC packs. I'm pretty sure you can do this on PlayStation and I think this would be a great feature to add. Now I'm not sure if this is to ever be included that a game's 100% achievement completion should count only from the base games achievements or the base game and the DLC achievements. I think the 100% should count from the base game and the DLC. Even though I do find it annoying when I've got 100% on a game and then find out a few weeks later that they've added even more achievements so my 100% is no longer valid. I think my view of that feature is subject to change. Maybe getting a badge for 100% all base game achievements and maybe mini badges for the DLC achievements might be a good idea. I should have a look into how PlayStation does it but either way categorising the achievements by base game and DLC will be a well welcomed feature.
